Hongkang Tao

School of Mechanical Science and Engineering, Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Wuhan, China

I entered the School of Mechanical Science & Engineering at Huazhong University of Science and Technology in 2025 to pursue a Ph.D. degree.

My research interests include object detection, perception and decision-making for autonomous driving in complex off-road environments, as well as neural network compression and lightweight model design. I am committed to developing highly robust object detection and multimodal perception methods for unstructured and dynamic scenes, and I investigate pruning, sparsity learning, and structural optimization techniques to improve the deployment efficiency and real-time performance of perception models on edge devices and practical systems. In addition, I explore autonomous 3D scene reconstruction based on 3D Gaussian Splatting, with further extensions to UAV target tracking and cooperative air–ground perception and intelligent decision-making for unmanned ground vehicles and aerial platforms.
